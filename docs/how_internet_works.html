How The Internet Works

The internet today consists of multiple different pieces of computer and other devices communicating with each other through a network using a wireless and wired network. The Internet is formally defined as “a loosely-organized international collaboration of autonomous, interconnected networks, supports host-to-host communication through voluntary adherence to open protocols and procedures defined by Internet Standards.”

The internet speaks in bits and sends binary information in the form of bits. That is the technical language of the internet. All information on the internet is made up of bits and sent in bits. Bandwidth is the transmission capacity of an internet connection measure by bitrate. Bitrate is the number of bits per second a system can transmit and latency is the time it takes for a bit to travel from sender to receiver. In order to support data travel at certain speeds, certain equipment is needed since certain types of equipment and/or connections might have their limitations.

Ethernet cables that are powered by electricity may be a good and decent solution given that they are cheap and affordable. However, they show measurable signal loss and interference over the distance of 100ft. So, how can providers supply the internet to distant locations, across oceans for example? One answer is light. Light travels faster than electricity through a wire. Bits can be sent as light beams through fiber optic cables that are essentially threads of glass engineered to reflect light. However, times have shown that fiber optic cables are expensive and difficult to work with. Another answer could be a radio signal, however, this method has geographic limitations.

When using any wireless connection, this connection is only wireless to a certain extent. Bits are sent from your device to the router and then are transferred into the physical wire to travel the typical distances of the internet. Regardless of the technology being used today or the technology that will be used in the future, the underlying binary representation of the information and the protocols of sending and receiving this information have stayed the same. The 1’s and 0’s will always be there.

Today there are several types of connections that enable people to connect to the internet. The slowest type of internet connection today is the dial-up connection. Dial-up internet uses your phone line, so using the internet will prevent you from being able to use your landline and the internet at the same time. DSL service also uses the phone line to connect to the internet but does not require a landline at home. It also uses a broadband connection which makes it faster than a dial-up connection.

Another type of connection would be a cable connection. Cable service connects to the internet via cable TV, and also uses a broadband connection to connect to the internet. However, cable connections are only available where cable TV is available although it is not required to have cable TV to have a cable connection. It is also possible to connect to the internet via satellite. Satellite connection uses broadband but does not require a phone or cable line. The connection is established through satellites that are orbiting the earth. It can be used almost anywhere in the world, however, the connection can be affected by the weather very easily and the speed of satellite connections is usually slower than a DSL or cable connection.

Finally, we can also connect to the internet using 3G and 4G services. This service is most commonly used with mobile phones and tablets when WiFi is not available. It connects wirelessly through your Internet Service Provider’s network. These connections are not as stable as DSL or cable connections, since speeds can vary depending on network activity, and providers will limit the amount of data you can use each month, unlike other connections.

To be able to establish some of these connections, certain equipment is needed. You may require a modem, a router, a switch, or a firewall, and in some cases all four of them. A modem makes the connection to the Internet Service Provider. A router will direct the signal to and from multiple computers/devices to multiple internet sites. A switch is a device that connects different pieces of a network together. Lastly, a firewall is a hardware or software device that filters traffic from the internet to your computer to screen for malicious programs, also known as malware. However, the above-mentioned equipment is sometimes not enough for certain settings.

You may also need cables, NIC, Access Points, and Repeaters. Cables are copper cables or fiber optic cables, and they provide a physical means to transport data between devices. A Network Interface Card (NIC) is a device to connect a physical cable to a computer usually with an RJ45 connector, also known as an Ethernet port. However, if a physical connection is not being established, then you will need an Access Point or a Wireless Access Point (WAP). This is a device that allows wireless devices to connect to your Local Area Network through WiFi or Bluetooth. Lastly, if your network needs to cover a vast area, you will need a Repeater that takes a wireless signal and rebroadcasts it at higher power. This will strengthen the signal where there might be a physical impediment and ensure that you have a strong connection through and through.
